Mobile Grooming

Starting a mobile grooming van business in 2026

May 16, 2026 · 13 min read · By the PawDesk team

The appeal of a mobile grooming van is obvious: no commercial rent, a service area you define, a premium price point (clients pay for convenience), and the freedom to build a schedule that works for you. The reality is equally appealing — mobile groomers consistently report higher per-hour earnings and greater job satisfaction than salon-based groomers. They also report that the business side is harder to manage on the road without the right tools.

This guide covers everything you need to think about before and after you buy the van, from permitting to route optimization to keeping your client records organized without a fixed office.

Is mobile grooming right for you?

Mobile grooming suits groomers who are self-directed, comfortable driving a large vehicle, and capable of managing the full business themselves with no support staff. The upsides are real: premium pricing, no commute for clients (they'll pay $20-25 extra for that), and a genuinely pleasant working environment if you set up the van well.

The downsides are equally real: the van is a capital expense ($40,000-80,000 for a purpose-built rig), you're weather-dependent (parking and water access in Canadian winters is a genuine challenge), and every admin task — booking, invoicing, client notes — falls entirely on you with no receptionist or support. Mobile groomers who struggle almost always cite admin overload as the first sign of strain.

Van selection: what to look for

The base vehicle for a professional mobile grooming setup is usually a Ford Transit, Ram ProMaster, or Mercedes Sprinter. These high-roof vans give you standing room (critical for an 8-hour day), reasonable cargo capacity for equipment, and a platform that conversion companies know how to build out.

Purpose-built grooming vans from companies like Shaggy Chic, Wag'n Tails, or customized builds from local conversion shops include the tub, grooming table, dryer system, water tank, hot water heater, generator or shore-power hookup, and storage cabinetry. A complete new build runs $70,000-90,000. Used purpose-built vans in good condition go for $35,000-55,000 and are a solid choice for first-time mobile groomers.

Key things to check on a used grooming van:

  • Water system integrity — look for rust, leaks, and whether the hot water heater still works efficiently
  • Generator hours and condition — a generator that needs replacement is a $3,000-5,000 cost
  • Base vehicle mileage and service history
  • Tub and table condition — repairing fiberglass tubs is expensive
  • Dryer and HVAC system functionality

Permits, insurance, and business registration

Requirements vary by province and state, but most mobile grooming businesses need:

  • Business registration — sole proprietor or corporation depending on your situation. Consult an accountant about structure.
  • Commercial vehicle insurance — not personal auto insurance. Your personal policy won't cover a commercial vehicle used for business. Expect $3,000-6,000 per year depending on province.
  • General liability insurance — covers you if a dog is injured in your care. $1,000-2,000/year is typical for a solo operator.
  • HST/GST registration — required once you earn over $30,000/year in Canada. Register early to make bookkeeping cleaner from the start.
  • Municipal business license — some cities require a home-based business license even for mobile operations. Check with your municipality.

Route planning and scheduling for mobile groomers

Route efficiency is where most mobile groomers leave money on the table. Driving 45 minutes between appointments means you're doing 4 grooms a day when you could be doing 6. A well-planned route clusters appointments geographically, minimizing drive time and maximizing grooming time.

The basic principle: designate different days for different neighborhoods. Monday is the west end. Tuesday is the east end. Wednesday is downtown area. This way you're never driving across the city between clients, and marketing in each area is targeted — you're the mobile groomer for that neighborhood.

For client management on the road, a mobile-first app like PawDesk is essential. You need to be able to pull up a pet's profile, check their last groom notes, and generate an invoice from your phone before you even step out of the driver's seat. A desktop spreadsheet or a system that requires a stable internet connection is a liability on the road.

Pricing for mobile grooming

Mobile groomers can and should charge a premium over their salon-based competitors. The standard premium is $15-25 on top of equivalent salon rates. Clients are paying for the convenience of not having to transport their dog — that's worth real money to a busy professional or a household with a large, anxious, or reactive dog that's difficult to transport.

Additionally, because you're only seeing one dog at a time (no kenneling, no shared space), you can legitimately position this as a lower-stress, higher-quality experience. That's a real differentiator that justifies the premium beyond pure convenience.

The admin stack for a mobile grooming business

Running a mobile grooming business with no fixed location means you need every system to work from your phone, offline when necessary, and without requiring you to sit at a desk. The three critical admin categories:

  • Scheduling and reminders — you need to book appointments, avoid double-booking, and send automatic reminders so clients don't forget when you're coming. Manual texting doesn't scale past 15 clients.
  • Client and pet notes — in a salon you can walk to a filing cabinet. In a van, everything needs to be on your phone. Per-pet notes with shampoo preferences, allergies, temperament, and visit history are critical for providing a consistent experience.
  • Invoicing and payment — cash is declining. Clients expect to pay by tap or card, and they expect a professional invoice. A dedicated app that handles invoicing in one tap is worth far more than its cost in time saved and payment speed improved.
