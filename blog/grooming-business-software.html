Best apps for pet groomers in 2026: honest reviews

May 16, 2026 · 14 min read · By the PawDesk team

Groomers ask us constantly what software they should use. The honest answer is: it depends on what you need. This is a no-nonsense review of the most commonly used tools — what each one actually does well, what it doesn't, and who it's right for. We've grouped them by category rather than doing a straight ranking, because the best tool for a solo mobile groomer looks very different from the best tool for a five-staff salon.

Disclosure: PawDesk is one of the tools reviewed here. We've tried to be objective, but if you want a second opinion, ask in any groomer Facebook group — mobile groomers are very vocal about what they use and why.

Apps built specifically for pet groomers

Best for: solo groomers and mobile operators

Groomsoft From $49/mo
Good for: established salons

Groomsoft is one of the older purpose-built grooming management tools and it shows — in both the good and bad sense. It has a very comprehensive feature set built up over many years: appointment scheduling, client management, inventory tracking, point-of-sale, online booking widget. For a multi-groomer salon, it covers a lot of ground.

The downsides: the UI was designed for desktop and hasn't aged gracefully on mobile. The onboarding is complex. And at $49/month for the basic tier, it's priced for established businesses rather than solo operators who are just getting organized. If you're a busy salon owner with 3+ groomers and a front desk person, it's worth evaluating. If you're a solo groomer, it's overkill.

Best for: Established salons with dedicated staff who can manage a complex system.

MoeGo From $30/mo
Good for: client-facing online booking

MoeGo has strong online booking functionality — clients can book their own appointments from a widget on your website or a Google booking link. If having clients self-schedule is important to your workflow, MoeGo does this better than most. It also has solid SMS/text reminder functionality, which some groomers prefer over email reminders.

Where MoeGo falls short for many groomers: the per-pet profile functionality is less detailed than purpose-built tools, the mobile app isn't as polished as the web interface, and the pricing adds up quickly once you need more features. It's a good fit for a groomer who wants to automate client-side booking but doesn't need deep per-pet record keeping.

Best for: Groomers who want clients to be able to book themselves online.

Generic booking tools groomers adapt

Square Appointments Free–$29/mo + payment processing fees
Limited for groomers

Square is the most common "adapted" tool in the grooming world — groomers who already use Square for payments often try to use Square Appointments for scheduling. It's a competent general booking tool with good payment integration. The problem for groomers is everything it doesn't do: no pet profiles, no per-visit notes specific to the animal, no grooming-specific fields for coat type or allergies. Every appointment starts from scratch with no institutional memory about the pet.

The payment processing is Square's real advantage — tap-to-pay, instant fund transfers, and a hardware ecosystem (card readers) that works well for in-person payments. If payment processing is your primary need and you're already in the Square ecosystem, it may make sense to keep Square for payments and add a grooming-specific tool for client management.

Best for: Groomers already using Square POS who want basic scheduling on top of their payment system. Not a complete solution. See full comparison with PawDesk.

Acuity Scheduling From $20/mo
Limited for groomers

Acuity is excellent at online booking — arguably the best client self-scheduling experience in this price range. The intake form feature (where clients answer questions before booking) can be adapted to collect pet information, which is a creative workaround for Acuity's lack of structured pet profiles. Some groomers use Acuity for booking and a separate notes document system for pet records.

The friction is that this patchwork requires you to manually transfer information between systems and remember which document holds what. As your client list grows past 20-30 clients, it becomes unwieldy. Acuity also doesn't have an invoicing feature — you'd need to add a separate billing tool. See full comparison with PawDesk.

Best for: Groomers who primarily want client self-booking and don't need deep pet record keeping or integrated invoicing.

Spreadsheets and free tools

Google Sheets / Excel Free
Not recommended past 15 clients

We've covered this in depth in our comparison guide. Short version: spreadsheets are free and feel flexible, but they fail groomers in every area that matters — no appointment conflict detection, no automatic reminders, no structured pet profiles, no invoicing, and no way to search your records on a phone quickly mid-groom. The time cost of maintaining a spreadsheet past 15 clients exceeds the cost of any tool on this list.

Best for: Groomers with fewer than 10 clients who are just starting and aren't ready to invest in software. Everyone else should migrate.

The verdict: what most solo groomers actually need

The majority of independent groomers and mobile operators have the same core needs: a calendar that prevents double-booking, automatic appointment reminders, per-pet notes they can access on their phone, and a fast way to generate and send an invoice. Most of the tools reviewed here partially cover this list. None except PawDesk cover all of it in a single, mobile-first app at a price accessible to solo operators.

The grooming software market is in an interesting moment — older desktop-first tools are being replaced by mobile-first alternatives built by people who actually understand the grooming workflow. That's a good trend for groomers, and competition is driving pricing down. The best time to find a tool that fits your business and stick with it is now, before switching costs accumulate in the form of client history you'd have to manually migrate.
