Use cases

One app, four very different days

A mobile groomer's day looks nothing like a salon owner's. PawDesk adapts to how you actually work — here's the workflow for each kind of pet-care business.

🚐 Mobile grooming vans 🏠 Home & salon groomers 🐈 Cat groomers 🦮 Dog walkers & sitters
For mobile groomers

Mobile grooming vansLive now

A mobile groomer's enemy is dead time and dead signal. You're driving between appointments across town, parking in a stranger's driveway, and grooming a dog in a van with no Wi-Fi and one bar of cell service. The last thing you need is software that spins a loading wheel when you tap a client's profile.

PawDesk is built offline-first specifically for this. Your full schedule, every pet profile, and the voice-note recorder all work with no connection. You pull up to the house, glance at the dog's history and allergy notes before you even unload the table, do the groom, dictate a 15-second voice note while you sweep out the van, and tap to generate the invoice. Everything syncs the moment you're back in range.

The mobile-grooming day in PawDesk:

  • Route-friendly schedule — appointments listed in time order with the client address one tap from the maps app, so you can sequence your day instead of crisscrossing the city.
  • Offline pet profiles — breed, coat type, last-visit notes, and "watch the back left paw" warnings load instantly with no signal.
  • Hands-free voice notes — record what you noticed while your hands are still wet; PawDesk transcribes it once you reconnect.
  • Invoice from the curb — generate and send the PDF before you pull away, so payment chasing doesn't pile up to a Sunday-night marathon.
  • 24-hour reminders — automatic, so the driveway you drove 25 minutes to reach actually has a dog waiting.

If grooming on the road is your whole business, this is the use case PawDesk was designed around first. Read our full guide to running a mobile grooming van.

For home & salon groomers

Home & salon groomersLive now

When clients come to you, the pressure moves from the road to the schedule. You're running back-to-back appointments in a fixed space, often alone, and the difference between a calm day and a chaotic one is whether the next dog's owner shows up on time and whether you remember that this particular poodle hates the high-velocity dryer.

For a home-based or single-chair salon groomer, PawDesk replaces the wall calendar, the client text threads, and the shoebox of handwritten cards. You see the whole day at a glance, every pet's profile is one tap from the appointment, and invoices go out the moment a groom is done instead of being scribbled on a notepad to "do later."

The salon day in PawDesk:

  • Day and week views — block lunch, see gaps you can fill, and avoid the double-book that wrecks an afternoon.
  • Client & pet profiles — owner contact, the dog's temperament, products that work, and a full visit history so regulars feel remembered.
  • Before & after photos — attach them to each visit; useful for your own records and for showing an owner exactly what you did.
  • One-tap invoicing — a professional PDF lands in the owner's inbox before they're back in the car.
  • Grow when ready — if you add a second groomer, the Business plan adds separate calendars and a shared client list without changing how you work.

Solo and home-salon groomers run on the Free or Pro plan. Multi-groomer salons use the Business plan. Compare the plans.

For cat groomers

Cat groomersLive now

Cat grooming is its own discipline, and generic salon software ignores it completely. A cat's record needs different fields than a dog's: coat length and matting pattern, stress and handling notes, sedation history with the vet, whether this is a lion-cut regular or a one-off de-matting rescue. Getting handling notes wrong isn't an inconvenience — it's a welfare and safety issue.

PawDesk's pet-profile system is species-aware. For cats you record coat texture and mat-prone areas, temperament and handling approach ("towel wrap, no scruff"), the vet contact for sedation cases, and the exact groom style the owner wants repeated. Voice notes are ideal here — you can dictate "stress level was high after the sanitary trim, recommend shorter sessions next time" the moment the cat is back in the carrier, without fighting a keyboard.

The cat-grooming workflow in PawDesk:

  • Cat-specific profile fields — coat length and texture, mat pattern, declaw status, and handling notes that travel with the cat to every visit.
  • Temperament & safety notes — flagged prominently so the next groom starts informed, not guessing.
  • Vet & sedation history — keep the vet contact and prior sedation notes on the record for cases that need them.
  • Shorter-cycle scheduling — cats that need frequent maintenance grooms are easy to rebook on a recurring rhythm.
  • Voice notes — capture handling and stress observations hands-free while they're fresh.

Whether you groom only cats or run a mixed dog-and-cat practice, the same scheduling, invoicing, and reminder tools apply. See the FAQ on cat grooming.

For dog walkers & pet sitters

Dog walkers & pet sittersOn the roadmap

Plenty of independent groomers also walk dogs or pet-sit on the side, and many walkers add grooming add-ons. The day-to-day problem is the same one PawDesk solves for groomers: too many clients, too many recurring visits, and too much of it living in text messages and memory.

Today you can already use PawDesk for the parts that overlap — client and pet profiles, recurring appointments, automated reminders, and one-tap invoicing all work for a daily walk or an overnight sit exactly as they do for a groom. A dedicated walking and sitting mode (route check-ins, GPS walk logs, and visit reports for owners) is on the roadmap; we are honest that it is not shipped yet, so the homepage marks these niches as upcoming rather than claiming features that don't exist.

What works for walkers and sitters today:

  • Recurring appointments — set a Monday/Wednesday/Friday 8 a.m. walk once instead of re-booking it weekly.
  • Per-pet profiles — feeding schedule, medication, leash behaviour, and emergency vet contact in one place.
  • Automated reminders — owners get a heads-up before a scheduled visit.
  • One-tap invoicing — bill a week of walks or a multi-day sit in seconds.

If you walk or sit alongside grooming, tell us what you need — early-access feedback shapes which of these ships next.
